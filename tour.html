Tara Tour Stops

1. Entrance

Step onto the Hill of Tara, the ancient seat of Ireland’s High Kings.
For thousands of years, this landscape has been a place of power, ceremony, and myth.
What you are about to experience is not just history — it is the story of a kingdom.

2. St. Patrick’s Church

Here, tradition tells of a moment that changed Ireland forever.
St. Patrick lit a fire in defiance of the High King, challenging the old ways.
From this hill, a new belief began to spread across the land.

3. Banqueting Hall

Stretching across the hill, this great avenue was once the ceremonial heart of Tara.
Kings, warriors, and nobles gathered here for feasts, rituals, and displays of power.
It was a place where alliances were made — and broken.

4. Rath of the Synods

Within this ancient enclosure stood a centre of political and spiritual authority.
Encircled by wooden palisades, it marked a place of control, ceremony, and decision-making.
Here, the power of Tara was both displayed and defended.

5. Mound of the Hostages

This tomb predates the kings of Tara by thousands of years.
At certain times of the year, sunlight enters its chamber, connecting the living with the dead.
It reminds us that this hill was sacred long before history was written.

6. Teach Cormaic

This ringfort is believed to be the royal house of King Cormac.
A place of leadership, law, and legend — where decisions shaped the fate of Ireland.
From here, the High King would rule over the land.

7. Stone of Destiny

The Lia Fáil stands as the symbol of kingship at Tara.
Legend says it would roar when touched by the rightful king.
Here, rulers were crowned — and the voice of the land was heard.
